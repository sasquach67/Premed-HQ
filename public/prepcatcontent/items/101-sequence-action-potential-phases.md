Action Potential Phases

0/7 · 0:00

Put the phases of a neuronal action potential in the correct chronological order, from the resting membrane potential through stimulation, the all-or-none spike, and the return to rest. Track which ion channels open and close at each step., drag the steps into order, then check.

1

Threshold reached

A graded depolarization from incoming stimuli brings the membrane to about -55 mV. This triggers the all-or-none response by opening voltage-gated Na+ channels.

2

Peak of the spike

Membrane potential reaches roughly +30 to +40 mV. Na+ channels inactivate (their inactivation gates close), halting further Na+ entry at the top of the spike.

3

Resting membrane potential

Membrane sits near -70 mV, maintained by the Na+/K+ ATPase and a high resting K+ permeability through leak channels. Voltage-gated Na+ and K+ channels are closed.

4

Repolarization (K+ efflux)

Voltage-gated K+ channels are now fully open and K+ flows out of the cell, returning the membrane potential back toward negative values.

5

Return to resting potential

K+ channels close and the Na+/K+ ATPase plus leak channels restore the normal ion distribution, bringing the membrane back to -70 mV and ready for the next signal.

6

Depolarization (Na+ influx)

Voltage-gated Na+ channels open and Na+ rushes into the cell down its electrochemical gradient, rapidly driving the membrane potential toward positive values.

7

Hyperpolarization (undershoot)

Slow-closing K+ channels stay open slightly too long, briefly dropping the membrane below the resting potential (more negative than -70 mV). This corresponds to the relative refractory period.
