Hormone Classes

0/12 · 0:00

0 pts

Sort each hormone into its chemical class. Peptide/protein hormones bind surface receptors; steroids (cholesterol-derived) and small lipophilic amino-acid derivatives (thyroid hormones) cross the membrane to intracellular receptors; catecholamines are amino-acid-derived but act at surface receptors.

Peptide

Made from amino acid chains; water-soluble; bind cell-surface receptors via second messengers

Steroid

Synthesized from cholesterol; lipophilic; cross the membrane to bind intracellular receptors

Amino-acid-derived

Derived from a single amino acid (tyrosine): catecholamines and thyroid hormones
