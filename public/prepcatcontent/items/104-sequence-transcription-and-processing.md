Transcription & Processing

0/7 · 0:00

Eukaryotic mRNA is made in two overlapping phases: transcription (synthesis of a pre-mRNA from a DNA template by RNA polymerase II) and post-transcriptional processing (the modifications that convert pre-mRNA into mature mRNA). Put the events in the order they occur, from RNA polymerase recognizing the gene through the final processing step. Remember that the 5′ cap is added co-transcriptionally (very early, right after initiation), while splicing, cleavage, and polyadenylation finish the transcript before export., drag the steps into order, then check.

1

Addition of the 5′ cap

A 7-methylguanosine cap is attached to the 5′ end via an unusual 5′-to-5′ triphosphate linkage. This occurs co-transcriptionally, right after initiation, once the nascent transcript is ~20 30 nucleotides long, protecting the mRNA and aiding ribosome binding.

2

Addition of the poly-A tail

After cleavage downstream of the AAUAAA signal, ~100 250 adenine nucleotides are added to the 3′ end, increasing stability and aiding export from the nucleus.

3

Termination

Transcription stops and the pre-mRNA is cleaved and released from the DNA template and polymerase, completing synthesis of the primary transcript.

4

Elongation

RNA polymerase moves 3′→5′ along the template strand, adding ribonucleotides to the growing transcript in the 5′→3′ direction to extend the pre-mRNA chain.

5

RNA polymerase binds the promoter

RNA polymerase II is recruited to the promoter (e.g., the TATA box) by general transcription factors, forming the pre-initiation complex just upstream of the gene's start site.

6

Initiation

The DNA duplex is unwound to form an open complex and RNA pol II begins synthesizing RNA from the template strand; the polymerase then clears the promoter.

7

Splicing

The spliceosome (snRNPs) removes non-coding introns and ligates the coding exons together, generating a continuous protein-coding sequence; in eukaryotes much of this occurs as the transcript is still being made.
