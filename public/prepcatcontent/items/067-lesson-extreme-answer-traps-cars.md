# Extreme-answer traps (CARS)

Why the strongest-sounding choice is usually wrong, and how to stop picking it.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN

Spot absolute language
Match answer scope to passage scope
Default to the defensible choice

CONCEPTS

Extreme = unsupported

Words like always, never, only, must, prove, impossible demand the passage support a universal claim. Passages rarely do. The credited answer is usually moderate and hedged (often, suggests, may, tends to).

Scope-match the answer

A right answer can't say more than the passage does. If the passage discusses one author's view, an answer generalizing to "all critics" is too broad, out of scope, even if it sounds smart.

Pick the defensible, not the impressive

CARS rewards the choice you could defend with a line from the text, not the boldest claim. When two answers remain, choose the weaker, more cautious one.

HEURISTIC

When stuck between a strong and a moderate answer, the moderate one wins unless the passage explicitly states the extreme.

CHEAT SHEET

Flag: always / never / only / must / prove
Right answers hedge: often / suggests / may
Answer scope ≤ passage scope
Defensible beats impressive

COMMON TRAPS

·
Picking the answer that "sounds smartest".
·
Generalizing one example to a universal rule.
·
Bringing in outside knowledge the passage never states.
