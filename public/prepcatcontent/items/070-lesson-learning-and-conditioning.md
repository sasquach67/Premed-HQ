# Learning & conditioning

Classical vs operant, and the positive/negative grid that trips up half of test day.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN

Separate classical from operant
Decode any reinforcement/punishment example
Rank the reinforcement schedules

CONCEPTS

Classical conditioning

Learning by association. A neutral stimulus paired with an unconditioned stimulus (UCS) becomes a conditioned stimulus (CS) that triggers a conditioned response (CR). Pavlov: bell (CS) + food (UCS) → salivation (CR). Watch acquisition, extinction, and spontaneous recovery.

Operant conditioning

Learning by consequence (Skinner). Reinforcement makes a behavior more likely; punishment makes it less likely. The reward/penalty follows the behavior, unlike the stimulus pairing in classical conditioning.

The 2×2 everyone misses

Reinforcement vs punishment = does behavior go UP or DOWN. Positive vs negative = is a stimulus ADDED or REMOVED (not good/bad). Negative reinforcement (removing something unpleasant) still INCREASES behavior, it is not punishment.

Schedules of reinforcement

Variable-ratio (slot machines) drives the highest, most extinction-resistant response rate. Ratio beats interval for response rate; variable beats fixed for resistance to extinction.

THE RULE

Two axes decode every example: reinforcement/punishment = behavior up/down; positive/negative = stimulus added/removed. "Negative" never means "bad."

CHEAT SHEET

Classical = association (Pavlov) · Operant = consequence (Skinner)
Reinforcement ↑ behavior · Punishment ↓ behavior
Positive = add a stimulus · Negative = remove a stimulus
Negative reinforcement ≠ punishment (it still ↑ behavior)
Variable-ratio = highest, most extinction-resistant rate
Know: acquisition, extinction, spontaneous recovery, generalization

COMMON TRAPS

·
Calling negative reinforcement a punishment (it increases behavior).
·
Reading positive/negative as good/bad instead of add/remove.
·
Mixing up the CS (bell) and the UCS (food) in classical conditioning.
·
Thinking fixed schedules resist extinction best, variable ones do.

Lock it in, drill this with the matching game.
