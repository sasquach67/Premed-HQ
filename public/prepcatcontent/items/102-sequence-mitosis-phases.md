Mitosis Phases

0/6 · 0:00

Arrange the stages of the eukaryotic cell cycle and mitosis in the correct sequence, from DNA replication through the physical division of the cytoplasm., drag the steps into order, then check.

1

Prophase

Chromatin condenses into visible chromosomes, the nucleolus disappears, and the mitotic spindle begins forming as centrosomes migrate to opposite poles. The nuclear envelope begins to break down.

2

Anaphase

The centromeres split and sister chromatids are pulled apart toward opposite poles by shortening kinetochore microtubules. Each separated chromatid is now an individual chromosome.

3

Telophase

Chromosomes decondense, two new nuclear envelopes re-form around the separated sets, the nucleolus reappears, and the spindle disassembles, yielding two distinct nuclei.

4

Metaphase

Spindle fibers attach to kinetochores and align the chromosomes single-file along the metaphase plate (cell equator). The spindle assembly checkpoint ensures proper attachment before progression.

5

Interphase

The cell grows and replicates its DNA during S phase (G1 -> S -> G2). Each chromosome becomes two identical sister chromatids joined at the centromere, and centrosomes are duplicated.

6

Cytokinesis

The cytoplasm divides via a contractile actin-myosin ring forming a cleavage furrow (in animal cells), producing two genetically identical diploid daughter cells.
