## Body systems and lab math

The gas, membrane, and separation equations the bio section slips in.

### Gases and membranes
[A] = K_H · P_A← Henry law · gas dissolved is proportional to its pressure
Π = iMRT← osmotic pressure · i is the van t Hoff factor
E = E° − (RT/nF) ln Q← Nernst · resting membrane potential
→
More soluble gas or higher partial pressure means more dissolves. This drives gas exchange in the lungs.

### Separations
R_f = (spot distance) / (solvent front distance)← chromatography
→
Electrophoresis separates by charge and size. Smaller and more charged travels farther.
