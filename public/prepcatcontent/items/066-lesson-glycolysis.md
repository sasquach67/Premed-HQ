# Glycolysis

Glucose → 2 pyruvate, in ten steps. Know the irreversible enzymes and the energy ledger cold.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN

Order the ten steps
Name the three irreversible regulatory enzymes
Tally the net ATP and NADH

CONCEPTS

Two phases

Investment phase (steps 1 5) spends 2 ATP to split glucose into two G3P. Payoff phase (steps 6 10) makes 4 ATP and 2 NADH from those two 3-carbon units.

The three irreversible steps

Hexokinase (step 1), Phosphofructokinase-1 (step 3, the rate-limiting and main regulatory step), and Pyruvate kinase (step 10). These are the committed, regulated points.

Net yield

Per glucose: net +2 ATP (4 made − 2 spent), +2 NADH, +2 pyruvate. Substrate-level phosphorylation (steps 7 and 10) makes the ATP, no oxygen required.

REGULATION

PFK-1 is the rate-limiting step: activated by AMP & F-2,6-BP, inhibited by ATP & citrate. It's where the cell decides to commit glucose.

CHEAT SHEET

Net: 2 ATP, 2 NADH, 2 pyruvate per glucose
Irreversible: Hexokinase, PFK-1, Pyruvate kinase
Rate-limiting: PFK-1
No O₂ needed (substrate-level phosphorylation)
Happens in the cytoplasm

COMMON TRAPS

·
Counting gross ATP (4) instead of net (2).
·
Calling hexokinase the rate-limiting step (it's PFK-1).
·
Forgetting DHAP is converted to G3P before continuing.
