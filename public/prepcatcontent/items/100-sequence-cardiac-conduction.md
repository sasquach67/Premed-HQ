Cardiac Conduction

0/7 · 0:00

Order the steps of the cardiac conduction pathway, the route an electrical impulse travels to coordinate one heartbeat. Start at the heart's natural pacemaker and follow the signal until the ventricles eject blood., drag the steps into order, then check.

1

AV node delay

The signal reaches the atrioventricular node, the only normal electrical link between atria and ventricles, where conduction slows (~0.1 s) so atria finish emptying before ventricles contract.

2

Atrial contraction

The impulse spreads across both atria (via internodal pathways and Bachmann's bundle), depolarizing atrial muscle and contracting the atria to top off the ventricles.

3

Purkinje fibers

The bundle branches give rise to Purkinje fibers, which rapidly distribute the impulse throughout the ventricular myocardium.

4

SA node fires

The sinoatrial node in the right atrium wall spontaneously depolarizes, acting as the heart's natural pacemaker and setting the rhythm.

5

Bundle branches

The bundle of His splits into right and left bundle branches that travel down opposite sides of the interventricular septum toward the apex.

6

Ventricular contraction

Ventricular muscle depolarizes from the apex upward, contracting to eject blood into the pulmonary artery and aorta.

7

Bundle of His

The impulse passes from the AV node into the bundle of His (AV bundle), carrying it into the interventricular septum.
