# Amino acids

Structures, charges, and one-letter codes, the most testable 20 molecules on the exam.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN

Classify the 20 by side-chain property
Predict charge at a given pH from pKa
Recall one-letter codes fast

CONCEPTS

Group by side chain

Nonpolar (Gly, Ala, Val, Leu, Ile, Pro, Phe, Met, Trp), polar uncharged (Ser, Thr, Cys, Tyr, Asn, Gln), acidic (Asp, Glu), basic (Lys, Arg, His). Knowing the bucket predicts behavior.

Charge follows pH vs pKa

A group is protonated when pH < pKa and deprotonated when pH > pKa. The isoelectric point (pI) is the pH where net charge = 0, average the two pKa values that flank the neutral form.

Special players

Glycine: only achiral AA (H side chain). Proline: secondary amine, kinks helices. Cysteine: forms disulfide bonds. Histidine: pKa ~6, the main physiological buffer/charge switch.

CHARGE RULE

pH < pKa → protonated (carries the "extra" proton). pH > pKa → deprotonated. pI = average of the two pKa around the neutral species.

CHEAT SHEET

Acidic: Asp (D), Glu (E)
Basic: Lys (K), Arg (R), His (H)
Sulfur-containing: Cys (C), Met (M)
Aromatic: Phe (F), Tyr (Y), Trp (W)
Glycine = achiral · Proline = helix breaker

COMMON TRAPS

·
Mixing up Asn/Gln (amides, neutral) with Asp/Glu (acids, charged).
·
Forgetting His is ~neutral-to-positive near physiological pH.
·
Assuming charge without comparing pH to each pKa.
