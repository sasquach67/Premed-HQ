PCR Steps

0/4 · 0:00

Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) amplifies a target DNA sequence through repeated thermal cycles. Each cycle has three core temperature-driven steps, and the cycle repeats 25-35 times, roughly doubling the DNA each round for exponential amplification. Put the steps in the correct order., drag the steps into order, then check.

1

Repeat the cycle 25-35 times

The three steps are repeated. Because each new strand serves as a template in the next round, the target DNA amplifies exponentially (roughly doubling each cycle).

2

Denaturation (~94-98 C)

High heat breaks the hydrogen bonds between complementary strands, separating double-stranded DNA into two single-stranded templates.

3

Extension / Elongation (~72 C)

A heat-stable DNA polymerase (e.g., Taq) adds dNTPs to the 3' end of each primer, synthesizing a new complementary strand 5' to 3' along the template.

4

Annealing (~50-65 C)

The temperature is lowered so short primers can base-pair (hybridize) to their complementary sequences flanking the target region on each single strand.
